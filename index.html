The protocol

Not a product. Not another EHR. A neutral open standard.

Like TCP/IP — Medisyns is infrastructure. It doesn't replace provincial systems. It connects them. Any compliant system can integrate. No vendor lock-in.

⬡

Open protocol

Governed by Medisyns Foundation (non-profit). Any provincial system, insurer, or pharmacy network can integrate. The standard belongs to no single vendor.

🔒

Patient data stays off-chain

Hyperledger Fabric. Patient PII never touches the ledger — only cryptographic event hashes. Fully PIPEDA and ITSG-33 compliant by architecture.

📋

FHIR R4 native

Built to the international healthcare interoperability standard. Compatible with every compliant provincial system today and any future procurement.

📊

Continuous audit trail

Every transaction timestamped and logged. The same record that satisfies a regulator generates the monthly invoice. One source of truth.

🛡

Patent protected

Protocol architecture, smart contract logic, and billing layer protected by patent applications filed in both the US and Canada.

⚖

Dual-entity governance

Medisyns Foundation governs the open standard. Medisyns Inc. operates the infrastructure. Provinces deal with a neutral protocol, not a proprietary vendor.

For provincial health systems

The problems Medisyns solves for provinces, ministries, and health authorities

Whether you're a minister looking at budget leakage, a CIO evaluating interoperability, or a health authority managing coordination of benefits — Medisyns addresses the same underlying gap.

Budget & fraud

Unknown fraud losses draining public health budgets

Public sector prescription fraud losses are estimated at $550M–$2.75B annually in Canada — but no province has a real-time baseline. Medisyns doesn't model the fraud rate. It builds the infrastructure to measure it for the first time.

Interoperability

Provincial and private systems don't talk to each other

When a patient has OHIP and a private employer plan, coordination of benefits requires two separate systems to verify in real time. Right now that process is slow, manual, and fraud-prone. Medisyns is the protocol layer that connects them.

Audit & compliance

Pharmacy oversight is periodic, not continuous

Ontario pharmacies are inspected on average once every 15 years. Medisyns replaces periodic inspection with a continuous automated audit trail — every dispense event logged, timestamped, and cross-referenced across all connected parties.

Procurement readiness

FHIR R4 and ITSG-33 compliance built in from day one

Medisyns is designed to meet federal and provincial procurement requirements without modification. PIPEDA compliant, ITSG-33 certified, SOC 2 Type II, FHIR R4 native. No retrofitting required.
