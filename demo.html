What just happened — in plain English

Every step above runs automatically for every prescription filled in a province connected to Medisyns. No human reviews it. No paper changes hands. The audit trail is permanent.

Everything connected

The provincial registry, the drug plan, the prescriber database, and the pharmacy all talk to each other through the Medisyns protocol. Right now they don't — each one is a separate silo.

Fraud caught before it happens

The duplicate prescription scenario shows a fraud flag firing before the drug leaves the counter. Today, that duplicate would only be discovered months later — if at all — during a manual audit.

Every transaction is permanent proof

Each entry in the audit ledger has a cryptographic hash — a unique fingerprint that proves the record has never been altered. Any regulator or auditor can verify it independently, at any time.
